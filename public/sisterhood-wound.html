Do You Feel This?

Does another woman's success quietly trigger you? Do you keep female friendships at arm's length even when you long for closeness? Do you give everything to other women while struggling to receive? Do you expect betrayal before it even happens?

If any of these land somewhere in your body, you may be carrying the sisterhood wound. And you are not alone in this.

This is not a personal failing. It is an inherited fear, passed down from generations of women who learned that sisterhood was dangerous. The healing begins the moment you name it.

Where This
Wound Began

The Ancestral Story

Long before we were born, women gathered in community. Healing together. Raising children together. Holding each other in circle. Sisterhood was not just valued. It was essential. Connection, not competition, was the foundation of feminine life.

As patriarchal systems rose, the feminine was devalued and suppressed. Women were pitted against each other for male approval, resources, and status. During the witch hunts, trust between women shattered completely. Survival sometimes meant betrayal.

Women learned to stay silent. To not make waves. To distance themselves from their own power and from one another. Trust became dangerous. Sisterhood became a liability.

Generations later, that fear still echoes in our nervous systems. We absorbed a belief that there is not enough space for all of us to shine. That other women are a threat. That closeness leads to pain.

But beneath this inherited fear is something ancient and true. A memory that lives in our bones, that we were once whole together. That we thrived not in competition, but in communion.

A Personal Share

A feminine wound I carried for a long time was that I would put all of my power into another sister. I would celebrate their gifts, do whatever I could to help them rise, hold their vision even bigger than they could hold it themselves.

And yet I would not feel the same about myself.

I had to learn that I was giving my power away. That it would be much more healthy to give that support from a balanced place within, not from a place of giving everything while quietly starving myself of the same recognition.

Do you see the gifts, beauty, and light in your sisters but struggle to recognise it in yourself? If so, it is time to reclaim your power. It is time to see yourself as the powerful woman you so easily see in others.

What I have found through circle work is how incredibly healing it is to be truly seen, heard, and held without judgement by other women. Many women will say the circle is the first time they have felt it. The first time feminine connection has felt safe. That is the medicine of true sisterhood.

The Healing

Healing the sisterhood wound is not just about mending your relationships with other women. It is about reclaiming the sacred feminine within you, between you and others, and in the world.

I

Notice the judgement without shame. When you feel competitive or critical toward another woman, pause. Ask gently: what insecurity in me is being activated right now?

II

Celebrate her wins. Actively, consciously, out loud. Her success does not diminish yours. It is a mirror of what is possible for you too.

III

Reclaim what you give away. Every time you see a gift in another woman, say it to yourself also. She is a mirror. What you recognise in her already lives in you.

IV

Seek safe circle. The wound was created in separation and heals in witnessed sisterhood. Find or create small, intentional spaces with women where you can be honest without performance.

V

Let yourself be held. Receiving from women is part of the healing. Allow it. Practice it. It is as sacred as what you give.
