The Body's Response
Your body was built to survive.

Fight, flight, freeze and fawn are not flaws. They are ancient, intelligent survival responses built into your nervous system long before conscious thought existed.

When your brain perceives danger, your body reacts automatically. Hormones flood in. Your heart rate rises. Your muscles tense. You are ready to act. Our ancestors needed this for real, physical threats. You still carry the same wiring. But today the perceived danger is more likely an argument, a fear of rejection, or a painful memory.

"Your brain does not always distinguish between a real threat and an emotional one. It responds the same way, because to your nervous system, the feeling is real."

Over time, these reactions become conditioned. The body associates certain situations, people or feelings with danger and responds before you even have time to think. This is not weakness. This is a nervous system doing exactly what it was designed to do.

Understanding this is not about excusing your reactions. It is about bringing them into the light so you can begin to choose differently.

Recognising Your Reactions
Five ways your nervous system protects you.

These are instinctual responses, not flaws. Each one was developed to keep you safe. Tap each to explore.

Fight control · anger · defend+
You push back. Anger rises fast. You need to be right, to defend, to control. You may feel the urge to dominate, argue or judge as a way to feel powerful or avoid vulnerability.
What it hidesUnderneath every fight response is fear or unworthiness, something that felt unsafe, unseen, or unheard. You learned to push back before you could be pushed down.
Flight avoid · busy · anxious+
Constant busyness, overworking, overthinking, perfectionism. You keep moving so you do not have to feel what is underneath. Your nervous system is stuck trying to outrun perceived danger or discomfort.
What it hidesThe busyness is protection. You learned that movement was safety, but the distance also keeps you from the connection and rest you truly need.
Freeze numb · stuck · shut down+
You go still. Procrastination, numbness, dissociation, withdrawal. Freeze is often the body's last resort to overwhelm, like hitting pause when too much is happening at once.
What it hidesFreeze is not laziness. It is your nervous system hitting its limit. The path back is not force. It is tiny, gentle steps toward safety.
Fawn please · appease · disappear+
You say yes when you mean no. You shape yourself around others' needs to stay safe and stay loved. Driven by a deep fear of rejection or abandonment.
What it hidesFawning can result in loss of self, feeling invisible, or not knowing what you want outside of what others need from you. Your needs matter too.
Flop collapse · surrender · give up+
Complete surrender, a shutdown of body and energy. You might feel lifeless, utterly exhausted, or like giving up entirely. Flop happens when the system has been overwhelmed for too long.
What it hidesThis is the deepest layer of protection, arising from deep hopelessness or when no other response feels safe. Only gentleness reaches here.

Attachment in Relationships
How your earliest relationships shaped you.

Your nervous system is shaped not only by stress and trauma, but by how emotionally safe you felt in your earliest relationships, especially with caregivers. These experiences formed your attachment style: a relational blueprint for how you connect with others today. Tap each to explore.

Secure Attachment+
Formed when caregivers were emotionally available and consistent. You likely feel comfortable with intimacy, have healthy boundaries, trust others, and can regulate your emotions relatively well.
In your relationships nowYou feel safe being close and safe being alone. Conflict does not feel catastrophic. You can ask for what you need and receive care without it feeling overwhelming.
Avoidant Attachment+
Formed when caregivers were distant, disinterested, or emotionally unavailable. You may feel uncomfortable with closeness, rely heavily on yourself, struggle to express emotions, and push others away.
In your relationships nowIntimacy can feel suffocating. You value independence intensely, because connection once felt unsafe. The longing for closeness and the fear of it often live side by side.
Anxious Attachment+
Formed when caregiving was inconsistent, sometimes nurturing, sometimes rejecting. A strong fear of abandonment, craving reassurance, becoming clingy or codependent, worrying you are "too much."
In your relationships nowYou reach hard for connection and panic when it feels uncertain. You might over-give or over-explain, because losing someone once felt truly catastrophic.
Disorganized Attachment+
Formed when caregivers were both a source of fear and comfort, often due to trauma, abuse or neglect. Torn between craving closeness and fearing it, switching unpredictably between avoidant and anxious patterns.
In your relationships nowYou want love deeply and also fear it deeply. Connection can feel chaotic or confusing. This is not your fault. It is the nervous system's response to love that also hurt.
"Attachment styles are not personality flaws. They are learned safety patterns. And just like nervous system responses, they can be rewired with awareness and breath."

Questions to Reveal and Heal
A process to navigate your reactions.

First, understand this: no one is responsible for your emotional reactions except you. That sounds confronting. But it is also the most freeing thing you will ever hear.

"My reaction is my work. Their reaction is theirs."

Instead of going into blame or shame when you react, use these three steps to create space to respond:

1
BreatheThis reconnects you to your body and the present moment. It signals safety to your nervous system. Inhale through the nose for 4 · Hold for 2 · Exhale for 8.
2
Identify the emotionSay: "I feel..." Breathe with the feeling. Allow it to move through your body. Observe it, without trying to fix it or push it away.
3
Validate your reactionNot because you are right, but because acceptance softens the reactive state into awareness. Blame and shame keep you spinning. Validation allows you to go deeper.

When you learn to calm and accept your reaction, you can return to your body and connect with what you truly feel underneath it.

The Deepening
Going below the reaction to the feeling.

Our reactions are always connected to deeper wounds. Bring a recent reaction to mind. Use these questions to move below the surface. Take your time.

What is the reactive emotion you are experiencing?
Anger, fear, disgust, annoyance, blame, guilt...

What is the feeling underneath that reaction?
Sadness, grief, rejection, betrayal, abandonment, hurt...

When have you felt this before?
Let whatever comes up arise: a memory, an age, a person, a moment.

"As we feel safe to bring these moments into our current awareness, we can start the deeper healing."
Showing Up for Yourself
To run, or to show up.

Running out on ourselves only leads to distrust in ourselves. It gives us the opening to betray ourselves, to blame ourselves, which triggers your Personal Lie and the mind chatter of untruths that send you into a spiral of shame.

Not showing up for yourself can manifest in many ways:

·Quitting before you gave yourself a real chance to start
·Procrastination, avoiding action out of fear of failure
·Waiting for the perfect moment or to feel a certain way. An illusion that sets you up to fail
·Analysis paralysis. Remember: do not believe all your thoughts
Showing up for yourself is not about forcing yourself to do something. It is about honouring yourself and checking in on whether it truly serves you.

Showing up is not a destination. It is a continual choice to choose yourself.

It is not about showing up only when life is smooth. It is about showing up especially when it is hard. About forgiving yourself, as many times as you need.

Most importantly, the confidence that you can trust yourself to be there when you need yourself most. Because through showing up, you discover who you truly are.

No one will ever meet your needs as well as you. And no one can ever love you the way you need to be loved until you do it for you.
