Week 6 · Foundations of Self

Leave Shame Behind

Shame thrives in silence. It cannot survive being seen with compassion. This week we move through a ceremony — in five steps — to meet your shame and walk away from it.

Step 1 of 5 — Name It

What shame are you carrying right now?

Shame lives in the unsaid. Name it here — just for you. No one else will see this. Say the quiet thing out loud onto the page.


Step 2 of 5 — See It

The Sacred Presence Practice

Read this slowly. Say it out loud if you can.

"This is shame. I see you.
I won't push you away.
I won't fight you or fix you.
I am simply here with you."

Place one hand on your heart. Breathe. Let whatever is here, be here. You are not broken for feeling this. You are human.

Presence dissolves shame more powerfully than perfection ever could.

Step 3 of 5 — Breathe It Through

Let the breath move it through you

Shame gets stuck in the body when we hold our breath. Let these three breaths create space.

Press begin
Step 4 of 5 — Leave It Behind

The Movement Practice

Stand up if you are able. This is a physical ceremony — your body needs to feel the shift, not just your mind.

1
Stand where you are. Feel shame in your body — where does it sit? Notice it without running from it.
2
Take one step physically to the side. As you step, say: "I leave this behind. This is not who I am."
3
Now take one step forward. As you step, say: "I am lovable. I am worthy. I am safe to be me."
4
Stand tall in your new position. Take a breath. This is your body claiming a new story.
Step 5 of 5 — Claim Your Truth

You did something profound today.

You did not run. You did not suppress. You looked at shame directly — and you walked away from it.

I am lovable.
I am worthy.
I am safe to be me.
"Shame cannot survive being witnessed with compassion. Every time you do this practice, you make it smaller — and you make yourself bigger."
