Shame Work

When shame is unconscious, it runs our lives. But when we learn to recognise it — and meet it with presence — we interrupt the cycle and begin true healing.

This Practice

This is a four-part practice to identify your shame patterns, honour what's present, and release shame from the body — gently, compassionately, powerfully.

① Recognise your physical shame signals
② Identify where shame lives in your roles and identity
③ Sacred Presence reconnection — breathe it, name it, hold it
④ Leave Shame Behind — a physical release practice

Recognising
Your Shame Signals

Shame has physical symptoms. When you can recognise the signals in your body, you begin to catch it before it controls you.

Step 1 — Your Body Signals

Select everything that resonates. Which of these do you experience when shame is present?

Dry mouth Racing heart Tunnel vision Freezing up Shrinking posture Face flushing Wanting to disappear Chest tightening Throat closing Stomach sinking Sudden silence Avoiding eye contact

Learn your shame signals — they are the body's invitation to go inward.

Where Shame
Hides in Your Life

We often shame ourselves around the roles we hold — where we feel we should be more, do more, or be different. Let's gently bring that into the light.

Step 2 — Shame Around Your Roles

For each area below, write what you believe you should be. Notice where there's a gap between the expectation and how you feel about yourself.

Body & Appearance
Work & Performance
Parenting & Family
Relationships
Who I am as a woman

Where am I holding shame because of unmet expectations?

Sacred Presence
Reconnection

This is not about fixing yourself. It's about making space for what's there — and learning to be with it, not against it. Presence dissolves shame more powerfully than perfection ever could.

Step 3 — Breathe, Name, Hold

Sit comfortably. Place one hand over your heart, the other on your belly. Take 3 slow breaths.

—
Hands on heart and belly. Press begin.
Sacred Words
"This is shame. I see you.
I won't push you away."
Say this gently, three times.
Let your body receive it.

Notice the sensations in your body. Where do you feel tightness, heat, or pressure? Imagine breathing space into that area.

"I'm safe now. I don't have to carry this anymore."

Leave Shame
Behind

Now we move shame out of the body — physically. Every step forward is a step out of shame and into your future self. Stand up for this practice.

Step 4 — Movement + Breath Practice
🤍
Stand up. Close your eyes. Take a deep breath. Bring awareness to the story of shame — the one that lives in your body. Feel the sensation. Let it surface without judging it.
→
Take a physical step to the side — leaving the story behind you. Inhale. Feel the lightness.
↑
Exhale and take a step forward. With each step, say a new truth aloud:
"I am lovable."
"I am worthy."
"I am safe to be me."

Feel each step forward as a step into your future self.

✦
Shame Work Complete

You are lovable.
You are worthy.
You are safe to be you.

Every time you meet shame with presence instead of avoidance, you weaken its hold. You are not your shame — and you've just proved it.

"Presence dissolves shame more powerfully than perfection ever could."
