Presence

Something has activated you. Before you react — pause. This practice brings you from your head back into your body, so you can respond from presence rather than pattern.

The First Truth
No one is responsible for your emotional reactions except you.

Ouch, I know.

"My reaction is my shit.
Their reaction is theirs."

Instead of going into blame or shame when you react — use these steps to create space to respond. This is not about excusing what happened. It is about taking your power back.

Now — what has activated you?

Step 2 — You Are Seen
What you are experiencing is real.

Whatever is here — it makes sense that it is here. You do not need to have it figured out. You do not need to be further along. You just need to be honest about where you are — and you just were.

Now — what is this triggering in you?

Not the story. The reaction. What is the feeling that rises when you think about it?

Step 3 — Validate

Validating does not mean you are right — but acceptance helps soften the emotionally reactive state into one of awareness, so you can understand the deeper feeling underneath.

Our reactions are always connected to our deeper wounds. Understanding them is the portal to your healing — and the first step in becoming self-responsible for the emotions you carry.

Now — three breaths. In through the nose, out through the mouth. Let your nervous system come home.

Press begin
Step 4 — Underneath
Now we go a little deeper.

Underneath the — there is usually a softer, more tender feeling. The reaction is the surface. This is the root.

When you sit with the and breathe into it — what do you actually feel underneath?

Step 5 — Into the Body
Where does this feeling live in your body?

Place your attention inward. Scan slowly — head to toe. Where do you feel this? There is no wrong answer. Even "everywhere" or "I am not sure" is something.

Stay with it for a moment. Just notice. You do not need to fix it. Noticing it IS the work.

Step 6 — Going Deeper

You have come a long way already.

If you feel settled and safe enough — there is one more question available to you. It is the doorway to the deeper work.

You can take it — or you can rest here.

The Deeper Question
Where have you felt this before?

This feeling — the one in your body right now — it is not new. It has a history. It belongs to a younger version of you who felt this and did not have the words or the safety to fully be with it.

Let whatever comes, come. There is no wrong answer. Even an age, an image, or a sense is enough.

You did something courageous today.

You did not run from what you were feeling. You turned toward it. You named it, felt it, and found it in your body. That is the work. That is how we move from reaction to response — one breath, one layer at a time.

Sit with this question before you move on:

"If I respond from presence rather than reaction — what would I choose differently?"

Journal your reflections. Come back to this practice any time you feel yourself spinning. It is always here.
