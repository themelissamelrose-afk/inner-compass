Meet Your
Inner Teen

They have been protecting you for a long time. Today we begin to see them — not to fix them, but to finally understand them.

"Your inner teen is not a separate entity. They are you — just as all the parts of you that you show when you wear different masks are also you. It is easier to separate them to name the wounded parts so they can be healed."

This journey moves from your head to your heart. We begin with understanding, then breath, then connection. Take your time. There is no rush here.

Cast Your
Mind Back

Get comfortable. Take a slow breath. Let a moment in time come to you without forcing it.

Head — Remembering
What age in your teen years comes to you first?
Do not choose. Let it arrive. The age that comes first is usually the one that most needs your attention.
Head — Two Perspectives
What was going on outside of you at that time?
School. Home. Friendships. Family. What was happening in the world around her?
What was going on inside of you?
What did they feel? What did they need? What were they telling themselves about who they were and what they deserved?
Head — The Masks
What masks did they wear to fit in?
Your inner teen shapeshifted to adapt — to get love, to avoid rejection, to survive. They became who they needed to be in different rooms. What were those versions of them?
Think about: at school, at home, with friends, with family. Where did they show up differently? Where did they shrink? Where did they perform? Where did they become someone else entirely?
Head — What They Needed
What did they most long for that they did not receive?
Not what they said they wanted. What they really needed underneath it all. The thing they would never have admitted out loud.
When they failed or got it wrong, what did they tell themselves?
Notice the voice. This is the inner critic they built to protect themselves — high standards so they could not be caught out. But when they fell short, how did they speak to themselves?
Head — Reevaluate Your Values
How have your values shifted?
Rate each value from 1 to 10. First as your inner teen — how important was it then? Then as your responsible adult self — how important is it now? Notice the gap. That is where they are still in the driver's seat.

Arrive into
Your Heart

You have done the remembering. Now we move from your head into your body, and from your body into your heart. This is where we meet her.

Breath — Arriving

Place one hand on your heart. Close your eyes. Let this breath carry you from your thinking mind into the space where they live.

Heart to Heart

You are the adult. They are the teen. You are meeting them in their moment — not to fix, not to change. To see them. To hear them. To let them know you understand.

Heart — The Dialogue
In your mind's eye, create a space where they feel safe. Let them arrive. When you sense their presence, open the conversation.
Let them speak first. Do not jump in. Do not try to fix what they share. Simply listen. When they are finished — ask them: "How can I be there for you?"
"Stay in your adult self as you meet them. You are not going back into that teen. You are meeting them as the person who now understands what they were carrying."
Heart — Compassion
Seeing them now — as the adult who knows what they did not know then — how do you feel about them?
They did the best they could with what they had. They were not wrong for protecting you. They were trying to keep you safe in the only ways they knew how.
What do they most need to hear from you today?
Write it to them. Use their age. Speak directly. This is the letter they have been waiting for.
Heart — Your Promise to Her
What is one small thing you can do today to show them you mean it?
Not a big gesture. Something so small it feels almost too easy. That is exactly right. We build trust one small promise at a time.
Ask yourself: "What can I give myself right now?" Then ask your inner teen — there is always one aspect of them that knows the answer.

Practice Complete

You showed up for them today.

That is the beginning of trust. Come back to her. Keep showing up. Over time the separation between you dissolves — and you become whole.

Place both hands on your heart. Take one slow breath. Breathe them in. Let them know you will be back. They are no longer alone.
