Become
the Observer

Most of us have an inner dialogue running constantly — a stream of thoughts that shapes how we feel, how we act, and what we believe about ourselves. This practice helps you become the one who watches — not the one who believes every thought.

The Purpose

Begin to observe your thoughts and thought patterns throughout your day. This practice helps you recognise that you are not your thoughts — and opens the door to living more consciously.

① Notice what you say to yourself throughout the day
② Identify when your mind gets busier — and why
③ Return to your body to feel what was there before the thought
④ Ask: "How do I feel?"

Your Inner
Dialogue

Take a moment to honestly notice the voice in your head. Not to judge it — just to see it.

Step 1 — Observe Your Self-Talk

As you move through your day, are you supportive of yourself — or do you tend to put yourself down? Which of these sounds most familiar?

"I'm not good enough for this."
"I should have done that better."
"I'm proud of how I handled that."
"Who am I to want more?"
"I can do hard things."
"Why do I always mess up?"

Choose any that feel true. Then continue.

The Busy Mind

Pay attention to when your mind gets busier. What circumstances or triggers send your "monkey mind" into overdrive?

Step 2 — Identify Your Triggers

What sends your mind spiralling? Select everything that resonates.

Conflict with others Financial stress Being alone Rejection or criticism Making decisions Late at night First thing in the morning When I feel ignored Comparison to others Uncertainty about the future When I feel out of control Scrolling social media

Bring it Into
the Light

Write down your observations. When you name something, it loses power over you. What was unconscious becomes conscious — and that is where freedom begins.

Step 3 — Your Observations

What recurring thought patterns have you noticed? Are they positive or negative, destructive or supportive? Write freely — without editing yourself.

Begin to reveal the inner dialogue that once operated unconsciously. By bringing it into your conscious awareness, you take back your power.

Return to
Your Body

After noting your thoughts, we always come back to the body. Because before your thoughts began, a feeling was activated here.

Step 4 — The Body Check

Our natural protection is to jump into thought — but now that you have observed it, consciously ask yourself:

"How do I feel?"
Not what you're thinking. Not the story.
The feeling. Right now. In your body.

Take a breath. Place one hand on your chest. Notice whatever sensation is present — and simply let it be.

✦
Practice Complete

You are not your thoughts.
You are the one who watches.

Every time you observe a thought rather than becoming it, you create a moment of freedom. That space — between thought and reaction — is where your power lives.

Carry This With You

Throughout today, notice your thoughts. Observe them like clouds moving through the sky. Name them: "There is the not-enough thought. There is the worry thought."

Then ask: "How do I feel?"
