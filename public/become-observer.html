Become
the Observer

Your thoughts are not the truth. They are either guiding you forward or protecting you from a feeling. This practice helps you tell the difference.

The Observer's Framework

Every time a thought arises, we ask the same four questions. Not to analyse the thought — but to feel beneath it. Because beneath every thought is a feeling that came first.

① What is the thought?
② What does this thought make me feel?
③ Breathe back into the body
④ Is this thought guiding me or protecting me?

What Is
the Thought?

Pause. Notice the voice in your head right now — or one that visits you often. Not to judge it. Just to see it clearly.

Step 1 — Name the Thought

Which of these sounds most like something you say to yourself?

"I'm not good enough for this."
"I should have done that better."
"Who am I to want more?"
"Why do I always mess up?"
"I need to do more before I can rest."
"I can't trust people to stay."

Choose what feels true right now. Then continue.

What Does This
Thought Make
You Feel?

The thought is not the origin. Beneath it is a feeling that was activated first. The thought rose to protect you from it — or to point you toward it.

Step 2 — Feel Beneath the Thought

When this thought arrives, what stirs inside you? Select everything that is present.

Anxious Afraid Ashamed Small Unseen Sad Angry Unworthy Lonely Overwhelmed Stuck Disconnected

You don't need to fix or explain this feeling. Just name it and let it be seen.

Breathe Back
Into Your Body

Before we ask what this thought is doing, we return to the body. Feelings live here — not in the mind. Let the breath bring you home.

Step 3 — Return to the Body

Place one hand on your chest. Follow the breath down. Let the body soften as you inhale and release as you exhale.

Ready

Guiding or
Protecting?

Now that you have felt what is underneath the thought, you can ask: what is this thought actually doing?

Step 4 — The Discernment

A thought that is guiding you points toward truth, growth, or honest emotion — even when it is uncomfortable. A thought that is protecting you keeps you away from a feeling that feels too big to sit with.

🧭
Guiding
This thought is honest. It is pointing me toward something real — growth, truth, or a need I have been ignoring.
🛡
Protecting
This thought is a shield. It is keeping me away from a feeling that feels unsafe or too overwhelming to touch.

There is no wrong answer. Protecting thoughts are not bad — they were formed to keep you safe. But recognising them is what sets you free.

When Does
the Mind
Get Loud?

Now that you know the feeling beneath the thought, notice what circumstances call that feeling up. These are the moments your protection activates.

Step 5 — Your Triggers

What sends your mind into overdrive? Select everything that feels familiar.

Conflict with others Financial stress Being alone Rejection or criticism Making decisions Late at night First thing in the morning When I feel ignored Comparison to others Uncertainty about the future When I feel out of control Scrolling social media

Bring It Into
the Light

When you name it, it loses power over you. What was unconscious becomes conscious — and that is where freedom begins.

Step 6 — Your Observations

Write freely. What did you discover beneath the thought? What is it protecting you from feeling?

You are not your thoughts. You are the awareness behind them. This practice, done again and again, is how you come home to yourself.

✦
Practice Complete

You are not your thoughts.
You are the one who watches.

Every time you go beneath a thought to find the feeling, you choose consciousness over protection. That space — between thought and reaction — is where your power lives.
