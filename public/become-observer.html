Become
the Observer

Your thoughts are not the truth. They are either guiding you forward or protecting you from a feeling. This practice helps you tell the difference.

The Observer's Framework

Every time a thought arises, we ask the same four questions. Not to analyse the thought — but to feel beneath it. Because beneath every thought is a feeling that came first.

① What is the thought?
② What does this thought make me feel?
③ Breathe back into the body
④ Is this thought guiding me or protecting me?

Some days you will observe and that will be enough. Other days you will want to go deeper. This practice meets you exactly where you are.

Now that you have felt what is underneath the thought, you can ask: what is this thought actually doing?

Step 4 — The Discernment

A thought that is guiding you points toward truth, growth, or honest emotion. A thought that is protecting you keeps you away from a feeling that feels too big to sit with.

🧭
Guiding
This thought is honest. It is pointing me toward something real — growth, truth, or a need I have been ignoring.
🛡
Protecting
This thought is a shield. It is keeping me away from a feeling that feels unsafe or too overwhelming to touch.
Not sure which one?
Ask yourself: if I removed this thought, what feeling would be right underneath it? If that feeling feels threatening — this thought is likely protecting. If the feeling feels like relief or truth — it is guiding.

There is no wrong answer. Protecting thoughts were formed to keep you safe. Recognising them is what sets you free.

Hand on Heart
Connect to yourself with warmth.
Place your right hand on your heart. Feel it beating. This is you — alive, present, here.
Breathe in slowly, feeling your chest rise beneath your hand.
As you exhale, let your shoulders drop. Let your jaw soften.
Say to yourself quietly: "I am here. I am safe. I am enough."
Repeat three times, breathing between each.
🌿
Ground Through Your Feet
Feel the earth. Feel yourself here.
Feel the floor beneath your feet. Press gently down. Notice the ground holding you.
Take a slow breath in through your nose. Feel your belly rise.
Exhale through your mouth. Let it all go.
Notice your seat. Your back. Your hands. You are here. You are real. You are held.
Take three more slow breaths. With each one, feel yourself becoming more present.
👁
Body Scan
Move slowly from head to feet.
Close your eyes. Take a slow breath. Begin at the top of your head.
Slowly scan down — face, jaw, neck, shoulders. Notice any tension. Breathe into it. Release.
Continue — chest, belly, arms, hands. Notice. Breathe. Release.
Down to hips, legs, knees, calves, feet. With each exhale, let the body soften.
When you reach your feet, take one full breath and open your eyes.
