The Abandonment
Wound

It is rarely just about being left. It is about what your nervous system learned love meant.

It is the child within who learned that connection could disappear. That affection could be withdrawn. That love might be inconsistent, conditional, or unsafe. That your needs might make you too much. That closeness can turn to distance without warning.

So you adapted. You became hyper-aware of people's energy shifts. You over-analysed texts and tone changes. You over-gave and over-functioned. You became who others needed you to be — in order to feel chosen.

Do You Recognise
Yourself Here?

This is not a diagnosis. It is an invitation to see yourself — with compassion, not judgement. Select what resonates.

Patterns of the Abandonment Wound

Whatever you selected — it makes sense. It was survival. It was learned.

The Paradox
at the Centre

Here is what most people don't realise — and what changes everything when they do.

The Deepest Truth
The surface fear
The abandonment wound fears being left by others.
↓
The deeper pattern
Its deepest pattern is often self-abandonment.

Because when we fear losing connection, we will often betray ourselves to keep it. We silence our needs. We tolerate what hurts us. We choose others over ourselves — again and again.

The person we most need to stop leaving is ourselves.

What Healing
Looks Like

Healing this wound is not what many people think it is. Let's name what it is — and what it isn't.

Healing is NOT
✕
Finding someone who never triggers your fear
✕
Securing perfect, unconditional love
✕
Guaranteeing you will never be left again
Healing IS
✦
Learning to stay with yourself when the fear rises
✦
Soothing your nervous system instead of spiralling
✦
Choosing self-respect over self-betrayal
The Healing Path
1
Honouring your needs instead of suppressing them — even when it feels safer to stay silent.
2
Trusting that being alone does not mean you are unlovable. Your worth is not tied to whether someone stays.
3
Knowing that someone leaving is not proof of your worth. It is information about the relationship — not about you.
4
Soothing your nervous system in the moment — instead of reaching outward for reassurance.
5
Choosing self-respect over self-betrayal — one small act at a time.

The Work

Read this slowly. Let it land in your body, not just your mind.

The work is not to stop others leaving you.

The work is to stop leaving yourself.
"I stay with myself, even when it is hard."
"My needs are valid. My voice matters."
"Being alone does not mean I am unlovable."
"I choose self-respect. I choose myself."
"I am worthy of the love I give to others."
