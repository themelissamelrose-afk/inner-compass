Your Inner
Seasons

You are not built for a 24-hour cycle. You move through four distinct inner seasons every month, each with its own energy, needs, gifts, and medicine. Learning to live with your cycle rather than against it changes everything.

🌕 You are currently in

Your cycle has changed. Your rhythm has not.

In perimenopause and menopause, the monthly bleed is no longer your guide. But you are not without rhythm. The moon becomes your external cycle, your external pulse. New moon to full moon mirrors the first half of a cycle. Full moon to new moon mirrors the second. Your body still responds to light, to season, to the cyclical nature of life.

This is not a loss. It is an initiation into a new kind of power. The wise woman, the elder, the one who no longer gives her blood to the earth but keeps it — her wisdom, her vitality, her creative force — within herself.

Your Four Moon Seasons

🌑
New Moon — Inner Winter
Days 1–7 of lunar cycle · time of rest and release
▾
ReflectiveInwardSensitiveIntuitiveLow energy

The new moon is your inner winter. The sky is dark. Your energy follows. You may feel withdrawn, contemplative, or emotionally tender during this phase — and in perimenopause where emotions already run close to the surface, this can feel intense.

What is happening: The absence of moonlight signals rest to the body. Combined with peri/menopausal hormonal fluctuation, the new moon window often brings the most inward, sensitive energy of the lunar month.

What this is asking of you: Rest without guilt. Journal. Release what is not working. Set no new intentions yet — simply clear space. Sleep more if you can. Say no to anything non-essential.

The gift: Your intuition is sharpest in the dark. What do you know but have been avoiding? This is the time it surfaces.

🌒
Waxing Moon — Inner Spring
Days 7–14 · growing light, rising energy
▾
MotivatedClearerCreativeOptimisticSocial

As the moon grows in the sky, your energy rises with it. The fog of the new moon window lifts. You may notice more motivation, more clarity, more appetite for connection and creativity.

What is happening: Light increases and so does your vitality. In this phase women in peri/menopause often experience their clearest, most grounded window of the lunar month.

What this is asking of you: Begin. Plan. Create. Say yes to things you declined in your new moon rest. Let the rising energy carry you into new action. This is your most productive and optimistic phase.

The gift: Whatever you begin here has momentum behind it. Ideas seeded in the new moon come to life in the waxing.

🌕
Full Moon — Inner Summer
Days 14–17 · peak light, full expression
▾
HeightenedExpressivePassionateMagneticPossibly overwhelmed

The full moon is peak energy, peak emotion, peak expression. For women in peri/menopause, this window can feel amplified — the emotional heightening that goes with the full moon combines with already-elevated hormonal sensitivity. Both the highs and the lows feel bigger here.

What is happening: Maximum light stimulates maximum energy. Emotions run closest to the surface. Sleep may be more disrupted. You may feel particularly alive, or particularly overwhelmed. Both are valid.

What this is asking of you: Express, connect, be seen — but also resource yourself. Avoid conflict where possible as emotional reactivity is highest. Use the full moon ritual. Sleep is especially important to protect right now.

The gift: Your voice is fullest, your presence most magnetic. This is your window for visibility, for leading, for being seen.

🌘
Waning Moon — Inner Autumn
Days 17–29 · releasing light, turning inward
▾
ReflectiveDiscerningCompletion-focusedNestingTruth-telling

As the moon wanes and the light decreases, your energy begins to turn inward again. The desire to complete, to declutter, to simplify and nest is natural and wise in this phase.

What is happening: The body follows the light. Energy reduces gradually. Emotional sensitivity may return, but it comes with the gift of discernment — things that are not working become very clear.

What this is asking of you: Complete what you began in the waxing and full moon. Finish rather than start. Reduce social obligations. Give yourself more solitude. Prepare for the new moon rest coming.

The gift: Your truth-telling is sharpest here. What needs to change in your life is impossible to ignore. Let this clarity inform your new moon intentions.

Support for This Stage

Perimenopause and menopause are not illnesses. They are a profound hormonal transition that deserves real understanding — not just management. Here is what is happening in your body, and why your moods and energy are shifting in the ways they are.

  • Hot flushes and night sweats — oestrogen fluctuation triggers the hypothalamus (your temperature regulator) to misfire. Your body is not broken. It is recalibrating.
  • Mood swings, anxiety, rage, tears — progesterone is your calming hormone. As it declines in perimenopause, oestrogen fluctuates wildly and the nervous system loses its buffer. Emotions feel closer to the surface because they are.
  • Brain fog and memory changes — oestrogen supports brain function, particularly memory and verbal processing. As it fluctuates, so does cognitive clarity. This is temporary and improves after menopause stabilises.
  • Sleep disruption — progesterone supports deep sleep. As it declines and hot flushes begin, sleep fragments. Prioritising sleep hygiene becomes one of the most powerful things you can do.
  • Irregular cycles, heavy periods, skipping — ovulation becomes irregular as the ovaries wind down. Some months you ovulate, some you don't. This creates cycles that are unpredictable in length, flow, and intensity.
  • Joint pain, fatigue, low libido — all connected to declining oestrogen and testosterone. These are real, not imagined, and they respond to lifestyle support.
  • The emotional depth — many women describe a clarity that comes with this transition. A dropping away of what does not matter. A rising of what does. This is the gift beneath the difficulty.
You are not falling apart. You are transitioning into a new season of your power. The wise woman does not have less to offer — she has more, with less tolerance for anything that does not align with who she truly is.
  • Phytoestrogens — plant-based compounds that gently support oestrogen receptors: flaxseeds, soy, edamame, tempeh, chickpeas, lentils. Particularly helpful for hot flushes.
  • Calcium for bone health — oestrogen protects bone density. As it declines, bones need extra support: dairy or fortified plant milks, leafy greens, almonds, sardines, broccoli.
  • Vitamin D — essential for calcium absorption and mood: sunlight, fatty fish, eggs, fortified foods. Most women in perimenopause are deficient. Consider supplementing.
  • Magnesium — reduces hot flushes, supports sleep, calms anxiety, eases muscle aches: dark chocolate, pumpkin seeds, leafy greens, almonds, supplements are well tolerated.
  • Omega-3 fats — supports heart health, brain function, and reduces inflammation and brain fog: oily fish, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds.
  • Protein at every meal — muscle mass declines with oestrogen. Prioritising protein supports muscle retention, metabolism, and energy.
  • Fibre for oestrogen clearance — the gut microbiome regulates oestrogen. A high fibre diet helps clear excess oestrogen and reduces oestrogen dominance symptoms: vegetables, whole grains, legumes.
Reduce alcohol, caffeine, spicy foods, and sugar — all are known to trigger hot flushes and amplify mood instability in perimenopause. These are not permanent restrictions, but reducing them for 4 weeks will show you clearly how much they affect you.
  • Strength training is the most important thing you can do — oestrogen protects bone density and muscle mass. As it declines, both are at risk. Resistance training 2–3 times per week is the single most effective tool against osteoporosis and metabolic change.
  • Weight-bearing exercise — walking, hiking, dancing, strength all build and maintain bone density. Aim for daily weight-bearing movement of some kind.
  • Yoga and Pilates — flexibility, balance, stress reduction, and sleep quality all improve with regular practice. Balance work becomes increasingly important in this stage.
  • Walking — one of the most consistently effective tools for perimenopause symptoms. Regulates cortisol, supports sleep, boosts mood, maintains bone density.
  • Be careful with very high intensity — long HIIT sessions spike cortisol, which is already elevated in many perimenopausal women. Moderate intensity, more often, is more effective than occasional extreme effort.
  • Recovery takes longer now — honour it. Adequate rest between sessions is not optional, it is part of the training.
The goal is not the same body you had at 30. The goal is a strong, resilient, mobile body that serves you well for the decades ahead. That body is built through consistency, not intensity.

You can still benefit from seed cycling in perimenopause and post-menopause. Without a cycle to follow, you use the moon as your guide instead.

New Moon to Full Moon (Days 1–14 Lunar)
🌱1 tablespoon ground flaxseeds daily — phytoestrogenic, supports oestrogen receptors
🎃1 tablespoon ground pumpkin seeds daily — zinc supports hormone production
Full Moon to New Moon (Days 15–29 Lunar)
🌻1 tablespoon ground sunflower seeds daily — Vitamin E, anti-inflammatory
⚫1 tablespoon ground sesame seeds daily — selenium and zinc, supports thyroid
Flaxseeds are especially powerful in perimenopause — their lignans act as gentle phytoestrogens that help regulate oestrogen receptor activity, which can ease hot flushes and mood fluctuation. Give it 3 lunar cycles to feel the difference.

The Four Inner Seasons

Tap each season to explore the energy, food, movement, and seeds for that phase of your cycle.

❄️ Phase One
Inner Winter — Menstruation
Days 1 to 5 (approximately)
▾

Oestrogen and progesterone are at their lowest. Energy naturally rests. The veil between conscious and unconscious is at its thinnest. This is a deeply intuitive, visionary time when you allow it rather than fight it. Many women report their most profound insights come during their bleed.

Rest Reflection Intuition Clarity Truth-telling
  • Iron-rich foods to replenish what is lost: lentils, kidney beans, leafy greens, pumpkin seeds
  • Warming, cooked foods: soups, stews, broths, roasted vegetables
  • Dark chocolate (high cacao) — magnesium eases cramping and supports mood
  • Nourishing fats: avocado, coconut oil, olive oil, ghee
  • Ginger and turmeric tea for warmth and inflammation
  • Bone broth to replenish minerals
Avoid caffeine and alcohol — they intensify cramping and disrupt sleep during this phase. Raw cold foods are harder to digest when the body needs warmth.
  • Rest is valid, valuable, and productive — honour it without guilt
  • Gentle yoga and restorative poses: legs up the wall, child's pose, supported supine positions
  • Slow walking in nature — the rhythm soothes the nervous system
  • Stretching and breathwork
  • Avoid intense cardio or heavy lifting — this can increase blood loss and deepen depletion
Listen to the body above every other instruction. If it says rest, rest. There is no power in pushing through this phase. Recovery comes faster when you honour it.
Days 1–14 Seed Protocol
🌱1 tablespoon ground flaxseeds daily — supports oestrogen regulation and metabolism
🎃1 tablespoon ground pumpkin seeds daily — zinc supports progesterone production for the next phase
Seeds must be ground (not whole) for nutrients to absorb. Add to smoothies, porridge, yoghurt, or salads. Store ground seeds in the fridge.
  • Review the month that has passed — what worked, what didn't, what do you want differently
  • Journal, dream, and set intentions for the cycle ahead
  • Schedule minimal obligations — protect this time for yourself
  • Cancel what doesn't feel aligned — your discernment is sharp right now
  • Avoid making big decisions or commitments from this state
🌿 Phase Two
Inner Spring — Follicular
Days 6 to 13 (approximately)
▾

Oestrogen begins to rise. Follicles mature. Energy returns gradually and then steadily. The body and mind begin to open. New ideas feel exciting and possible. Motivation rebuilds. This is the best phase for starting new things, learning, and saying yes.

Optimism Creativity Curiosity New beginnings Learning
  • Light, fresh foods: salads, sprouts, fresh vegetables, smoothies, fresh herbs
  • Fermented foods to support gut health which influences hormone balance: sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, natural yoghurt
  • Eggs — support follicle development
  • Lean proteins: chicken, fish, legumes, tofu
  • Fresh herbs: parsley, basil, mint, coriander
  • Seeds and nuts as snacks
The body tolerates lighter, rawer foods better in this phase. The gut is more receptive. This is a good time to reduce heavy, processed, or inflammatory foods.
  • Increase intensity — the body is ready and will respond well
  • Strength training: this is a great phase to lift heavier and build muscle
  • Cardiovascular training: running, cycling, swimming, dance classes
  • Try something new — the follicular energy loves novelty
  • Recovery is faster in this phase — you can push more

☀️ Phase Three
Inner Summer — Ovulation
Days 14 to 17 (approximately)
▾

Oestrogen peaks. LH surges and triggers ovulation. Energy is at its highest. The body is magnetic, warm, and open. This is the time when women often feel most themselves, most alive, most connected. The voice is strongest. Presence is fullest. Use it well.

Confidence Magnetism Communication Visibility Connection
  • Anti-inflammatory foods: berries, leafy greens, oily fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel), walnuts

  • Fresh fruits and salads — the body tolerates raw foods well now
  • Hydration is especially important around ovulation
  • Light, energising meals rather than heavy ones
  • Peak performance phase — train at your highest intensity here
  • HIIT, heavy lifting, long runs, competitive sport
  • Group fitness classes — you are most social and energised now
  • Anything that feels alive and challenging
  • Recovery is excellent — this is not the time to hold back

🍂 Phase Four
Inner Autumn — Luteal
Days 18 to 28 (approximately)
▾

Progesterone rises then falls. Oestrogen declines. The body prepares to shed. The inner critic can get louder in the final days — this is the PMS window. But this phase also has a profound gift: it strips away pretence. What is not working becomes impossible to ignore. Boundaries become clearer. Use the discernment.

Discernment Completion Nesting Detail-focus Truth-telling
  • Complex carbohydrates to stabilise blood sugar and support serotonin: sweet potato, brown rice, oats, legumes
  • Magnesium-rich foods to reduce PMS, cramping, and anxiety: dark leafy greens, pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate, almonds, bananas
  • Omega-3 fats to reduce inflammation and support mood: salmon, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseed oil
  • Calcium: dairy or fortified plant milks, sardines, broccoli, almonds
  • Warming, cooked foods as the body moves toward winter
Reduce caffeine, alcohol, and sugar especially in days 22 to 28 — sensitivity is heightened and they amplify mood instability. Stable blood sugar is the single most effective tool for reducing PMS.
  • Moderate, sustainable movement: pilates, yoga, strength at medium intensity
  • Long walks — especially in nature — ground the nervous system
  • Swimming is deeply soothing in this phase
  • In the final days (22 to 28) let the body guide you — it may ask for gentleness
  • Movement helps with progesterone balance and genuinely reduces PMS
This is not a rest phase — but it is not a peak phase either. Moderate, consistent movement is the sweet spot.

Seed Cycling at a Glance

Seed cycling uses the natural lignans, zinc, selenium, and vitamin E in four specific seeds to support your body's hormone production at each phase of the cycle. It is gentle, food-based hormone support that works with your body rather than overriding it.

Days 1 to 14 — Follicular Seeds

🌱Ground flaxseeds — 1 tbsp daily
Regulates oestrogen, supports hormone metabolism
🎃Ground pumpkin seeds — 1 tbsp daily
Zinc supports progesterone in the phase ahead

Days 15 to 28 — Luteal Seeds

🌻Ground sunflower seeds — 1 tbsp daily
Vitamin E supports corpus luteum and progesterone
⚫Ground sesame seeds — 1 tbsp daily
Selenium supports hormone production and thyroid

Add to smoothies, porridge, yoghurt, salads, or nut butter. Seeds must be ground for absorption. Store ground seeds in the fridge. Allow 3 full cycles to notice the shift.

Plan Your Month Around Your Cycle

When you know what phase is coming, you can schedule with your biology instead of against it. This is not rigid planning. It is intelligent alignment.

❄️ Inner Winter

Rest, journalling, reviewing, dreaming. Protect this time. Schedule nothing if you can.

🌿 Inner Spring

New projects, learning, first meetings, creative work, networking. Say yes here.

☀️ Inner Summer

Launches, speaking, visibility, negotiations, hosting, being seen. Your most powerful window.

🍂 Inner Autumn

Completion, editing, admin, honest conversations, nesting. Reduce social load at the end.
