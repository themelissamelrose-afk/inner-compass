This is not separate from your healing journey. It is a deeper layer of it.

What Is the
Witch Wound?

The Ancestral Story

The Witch Wound is an inherited, collective trauma rooted in the Burning Times. Tens of thousands of women and men were persecuted, tortured, and executed. Healers. Midwives. Wise women. Anyone too outspoken, too powerful, too different.

Our ancestors who survived carried this not just in memory but in their bodies. Their fear, shame, grief, and survival instincts were passed down through the ages. They now live in our blood and bones.

This is not just history. It is living in the patterns of every woman who has ever made herself smaller so others would not feel threatened by her light.

The witch wound and the sisterhood wound run deep in our DNA. It does not discount the inner child work. It is a deeper layer beneath it. When we follow the chord back through the patterns of persecution, being misunderstood, and betrayal, we find it playing through every relationship dynamic in our lives.

You Are Being Seen

The Grounding
Ritual

Move slowly through each step. There is no rush here. This is your time.

Earth Yourself
I

Come to the ground. Sit on the earth outside under the sky, or settle on the floor beside a window. Feel every place your body connects with what is beneath you.

II

Breathe three times. Deep breath in through the nose. Long exhale out through the mouth. Let the body arrive here fully before you continue.

III

Visualise the cord. Imagine a cord of energy, or roots, extending from the base of your spine down into the earth below you. Feel the connection.

IV

Send it down. With each exhale, send any pent-up energy, fear, or emotion that does not belong to you down through that cord and into the earth. She can hold it. You do not have to.

V

Repeat as many cycles as you need. Breathe in. Release down. There is no limit. Stay as long as the body asks you to.

Reclaim
Your Voice

Throat Chakra Activation

The throat chakra is where the witch wound lives in the body. The fear of being heard, misunderstood, or persecuted for speaking up. This is where we begin to reclaim.

I

Place your hand on your throat. Feel the warmth of your own touch there. This is a sacred part of you.

II

Speak your name aloud. Slowly. Three times. Let yourself be heard by yourself first. Your name is not something you should be afraid to own.

III

Hum or chant. Any tone, any sound. Let it vibrate in the throat. This activates and opens what has been held shut.

IV

Speak this aloud. Softly or with full voice. Either is enough.

"I am allowed to be seen. I am allowed to be heard. My truth is safe to speak. My gifts are safe to share. I am not my ancestors' fear. I am their healing."
