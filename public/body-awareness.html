Body-Based
Awareness

This practice will help you discern the difference between being in your mind versus being in your body — and cultivate a sense of internal safety and coming home to yourself.

"In accepting and acknowledging your emotional body, you allow for healing and expansion on all levels."

— Tracey Gibson

Throughout your day — or after breathwork — this practice invites you to consciously check in with your body. Notice. Feel. Listen. Let it guide you.

Find somewhere comfortable. You can close your eyes, or soften your gaze. Let this recording guide you home.

Guided Practice with Melissa
Body Scan
Listen with Melissa

Close your eyes. Take a slow breath. We are going to move through the body together.

Begin at the top of your head. Notice anything there. Breathe into it.

Move to your face. Your jaw. Let it soften. Your neck and shoulders. Breathe into any tension. And release.

Down to your chest. Your heart. Your belly. Notice what is here. Breathe in. And let it go.

Your arms. Your hands. Notice. Breathe. Release.

Your hips. Your legs. Your knees. Calves. All the way down to your feet.

Take one full breath from the crown of your head all the way to the soles of your feet.

And open your eyes. Welcome back.

What do you
notice?

After moving through your body, take a moment to observe what is present.

Observation Practice

As you breathe, consciously check in. Notice sensations — warmth, tension, openness, contraction. Let each feeling be welcome, without needing to change it.

What do you notice right now?

Warmth Tension Openness Tightness Heaviness Lightness Numbness Tingling Contraction Expansion Stillness Restlessness

Tap anything you feel. Then continue.

Let it anchor you into presence.

Body Question 2 of 3
What am I avoiding
feeling?
Your mind's job is to protect you. But the body holds what the mind moves past.
Is there something present that you've been bypassing?

You don't need to solve it. Simply be willing to see it.

Body Question 3 of 3
What message is my body
trying to communicate
to me?
Trust what arises — a word, an image, a colour, a feeling. Your body knows. Before your thoughts began, a feeling was activated here. Let yourself receive it.

Consciously ask: "How do I feel?"

Practice Complete

You are not your thoughts.
You are the awareness behind them.

Through this practice, you open the door to living more consciously — guided by the wisdom of your body rather than the noise of your mind.

Remember

Before your thoughts began, a feeling was activated in your body. Our natural protection is to jump into thought — but now you've observed it. You've asked: "How do I feel?"

That simple act is how you return home.
