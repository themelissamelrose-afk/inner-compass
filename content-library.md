## THE SOMATIC PROCESS — MELISSA'S TEACHING ON HOW TO GUIDE SOMEONE THROUGH A TRIGGER

I want to create some awareness around what they are feeling first. When someone shares a situation or circumstance, I like to validate that situation first. Maybe confirm what they are experiencing, then ask them: what does that trigger within them? What does that make them feel — what is their reaction to the circumstance, situation or person?

The reaction is the really important key. We need to address and get clear on the reaction, because the reaction is the portal back into the body. So if we're seeing the reaction — I'm really angry, I'm pissed off, I'm hurt, I feel betrayed — then we bring them back into the body.

Now we understand your reaction. It's valid to you. It doesn't mean it's right, but it's valid. If you are experiencing anger, validation creates acceptance.

Before we project our reaction, we bring presence and go within. We take self-responsibility first. Then we bring ourselves back into presence with the breath. We want the mind following the breath.

Take a nice deep breath in through the nose, deep breath out of the mouth. Do that three times. My feet are on the ground. My hands are beside me. We're bringing somatic awareness. We're bringing them into the present moment.

Now we address the feeling. When you feel angry — how does that feel? Because underneath the initial trigger there is a deeper feeling that lays underneath that. Then ask where does that feeling lay within your body? We're creating awareness of the feeling, bringing them back into the present moment, allowing them to find the sensation in their physical body.

And then, if they are calm enough, we ask: where have you felt this before?

Those are the steps. Sometimes we just have to get from the head to the heart and find acceptance that we're actually experiencing it, so we can view it from observation. Moving from reaction to responding differently.

A lot of people are stuck in their head so we need to work with what's visible first, observing their mind and the trigger first, then allowing them to bring awareness to the moment, then coming back to: what's the feeling under the trigger? Then giving them presence in the body. And if they can ask where they've felt this before, they can go a little deeper.

## THE DEEPER LAYERS OF SELF-ABANDONMENT

In the beginning, self-abandonment is obvious. Ignoring red flags. Staying when you're unhappy. Over-giving. People-pleasing. Accepting less than you deserve.

But as healing progresses, it becomes much more subtle. The next layer often looks like: doubting what you know, questioning your perception, giving old stories more authority than your present reality, looking outside yourself for confirmation when your body already knows, mistaking discomfort for a sign you're making the wrong decision.

The old thoughts still arise because they were wired into you for a long time. Healing doesn't necessarily stop the thoughts from appearing. It changes your relationship with them.

Instead of: "Maybe I'm asking too much." You now notice: "Ah, there's that old voice again."

Instead of: "Maybe I should stay." You notice: "I don't actually believe that anymore."

That is a huge difference.

What's beautiful about this deeper layer of healing is that you're not trying to get rid of disappointment. You're allowing disappointment without allowing it to dictate your choices. That's maturity.

For years, many women make decisions based on avoiding disappointment, loneliness, grief, rejection, or uncertainty. Eventually there comes a point where you realise: I can survive disappointment. What I don't want to survive anymore is abandoning myself.

Self-abandonment isn't always saying yes when you mean no.
Sometimes it's doubting yourself when you already know.
Sometimes it's negotiating with reality because the truth is inconvenient.
Sometimes it's giving old wounds a louder voice than present-day wisdom.

Healing isn't the absence of doubt. Healing is hearing the doubt and choosing yourself anyway.

The thoughts still visit. The wound still whispers. The disappointment still hurts. But you no longer hand them the steering wheel. You listen, acknowledge them, and then return to yourself.

That is a very different woman from the one who would have stayed hoping someone else would finally choose her. Now you're choosing yourself, even when it costs you something. And often, that's exactly where the deepest layer of healing lives.

## THE SURVIVAL ORIENTATION vs THE CREATIVE ORIENTATION

Most spiritually aware seekers do not realise they reverse their own creative power every time they try to manifest from a place of lack. Which is why the courses, retreats, and practices keep producing the same result: deep knowing, zero evidence.

You are driving home from the workshop. The one that cost $800 and a weekend. The one where you cried, connected, broke through something. You felt it — the expansion, the clarity, the knowing. For two days, everything made sense. And now, somewhere between the venue and the highway, you can feel it fading. By Monday morning, it will be gone.

The shelf in your office tells the story. Fifteen years of personal development. The meditation courses. The manifestation programs. The breathwork certifications. The shamanic journey. The Abraham Hicks workshops. The Landmark Forum. The vision board that has not changed since 2021. The journals — dozens of them — filled with intentions, affirmations, and insights that felt life-changing when you wrote them and mean nothing now.

You can articulate the principles of creation better than most teachers. You understand energy. You understand vibration. You understand the subconscious mind. You have read the books, done the practices, attended the retreats. You know more about manifestation than 99% of the population. And your life looks exactly the same as it did five years ago.

You carry it alone — the quiet, devastating thought that visits in the shower, in the car, in the space between sleep and waking: What if I am the one person this does not work for?

Here is what I have learned after 30 years and working with thousands of people: You are not the exception. The principles are real. And the reason nothing has worked is not what you think.

The problem is not your vibration. It is not your beliefs. It is not that you have not done enough inner work. The problem is that every practice, every course, every retreat has been operating from the same hidden structure. And that structure is the thing blocking your results.

I call it the Survival Orientation. Here is how it works in the spiritual world: You meditate to fix your anxiety. You affirm abundance to fix your scarcity. You visualise your dream life to escape your current one. You do breathwork to release trauma. Every practice — no matter how elevated it looks — is a reaction to something you believe is wrong with you.

Your subconscious mind does not hear "I am abundant." It hears "I am trying to fix my scarcity." It does not hear "I am creating my dream life." It hears "I am trying to escape this one." The power is in what you are running from. And your subconscious manifests accordingly.

This is why the workshop high fades by Monday. This is why the vision board has not changed. This is why you can articulate the principles perfectly and still not live them. You have been doing spiritual work from the Survival Orientation — and the Survival Orientation can only create more of what you are trying to get away from.

The Creative Orientation is different. It does not fix you because you were never broken. It shifts where your creative energy comes from. Not from compensation. Not from lack. From your true nature and purpose. From the vision itself — not as an escape from what is, but as an expression of who you are.

None of the other work was worthless. But none of it addressed the structure. None of it shifted the orientation. None of it questioned whether the place you were creating from was the actual problem.

## DAILY PRACTICES — BREATH AND PRESENCE THROUGH THE DAY

### 1. Morning Before You Even Open Your Eyes

Use the Sacred Six to tune into your body. Take a moment to just be there, notice any dreams, thoughts or feelings that are present as you wake. Don't try to change anything, just observe. Then use your exhale like a release, letting it all move through and out. From there, consciously inhale the day you want to begin with. Breathe in the energy of how you want to feel and gently meet the day ahead from that place.

### 2. Hourly Reminder — Breathe

Set a gentle alarm each hour that simply says Breathe. This is your moment to come back to yourself. We are interrupting the patterns of being lost in the mind, the distraction that keeps triggering your nervous system.

When the alarm goes off, pause. Notice the mind. What are you thinking? Just observe, see if you can witness what you're saying to yourself. If you can't yet, that's ok, you will in time.

Now take 3 deep breaths. Inhale through the nose, sigh it out the mouth. Bring yourself into your body.

My feet are on the ground — feel them.
My hands are beside me — feel them.

Now gently ask your body: what am I feeling? Just listen. And if something is there, let it be there. No fixing. No changing. Validate it. Allow yourself to be present with it. No judgement, just meeting yourself in presence. Without analysing. Without trying to work it out.

The feeling is just your current experience so allow it. Be with it.

If you feel nothing to begin with, that's ok too. Just let the feeling of presence itself calm your body.

This is how you stay grounded through your day, so you're not running on empty, and you begin to truly observe yourself.

## THE MORNING RITUAL — LIGHTKEEPER PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Protect Your First Hour — No Screens

When you wake up, we want one full hour with no emails, no social media, no messages, no scrolling.

You can use your phone only to press play on the meditation — but avoid notifications and communication. Even one notification can pull your brain into alert mode and fracture your focus for the entire morning. The goal is to keep your attention steady so your nervous system stays calm and your mind stays clear.

### Step 2: Hydrate with Intention

Before you do anything intense, hydrate. After sleep, your body is naturally dehydrated — and hydration supports circulation, clarity, digestion, and a calmer body state.

Start with a glass of fresh filtered water. If you want, do warm water with lemon and a tiny pinch of salt — it's gentle on the system, supports digestion, and the salt gives a light electrolyte boost to help the body absorb water more efficiently.

Or choose a morning tea instead of coffee and treat it like a ritual: slow sips, presence, intention — a gentle way to wake the nervous system without spiking it.

### Step 3: Light Movement

Give the body a few minutes of movement. Stretching, yoga, gentle movement — anything that gets blood flow moving. This is you signalling to your system: we're safe, we're here, we're alive. And that makes it easier to drop into meditation.

### Step 4: The LightForce Blessings Meditation — 40 Minutes, On Purpose

The Blessings Meditation is the foundation of the Lightkeeper path. It is intentionally 40 minutes because longer practices are more likely to guide the brain into theta brainwave activity — that gateway state where the mind becomes quieter, emotional material becomes more accessible, and the brain becomes more flexible in how it processes old patterns.

Short meditations can calm you. Longer meditations can rewire you. That's why we don't rush it.

When the guided meditation ends, the music continues for another 15 minutes. That is not "extra." That is one of the most powerful parts of the ritual. This is where you practise intention, visualisation, and emotional rehearsal — not just imagining what you want, but feeling it in your body. Because when you can feel it, you can hold it. And when you can hold it, you can become it.

This trains your nervous system to embody the frequency you're choosing — which supports both healing and manifestation.

### Step 5: Plan Your Day with the Daily Flow Report

After meditation, quickly plan your day using the Daily Flow Report. This helps reduce cognitive overload, increases follow-through, and gives you a way to track your emotional wellbeing as you move through the program.

The rule that changes everything: choose your top three must-do tasks and complete them before you open social media or email. That's how you protect your focus and stop your day from being hijacked by other people's priorities.

Commit to this daily for at least 30 days. That's how habits stabilise and the nervous system learns a new baseline. If you miss a day, don't spiral, don't shame yourself. You simply return the next day.

## BECOMING A FREQUENCY HOLDER

To become a frequency holder is to build the capacity to hold space without saving.

It is to witness the darkness in someone with understanding and compassion. To stay present with their pain without rushing to resolve it, fix it, or soften it into something more comfortable for you both to sit inside.

It is to hold an unwavering commitment to their reality. Not a version of it that makes them feel better right now. Their actual reality. The truth of where they are, what they are carrying, and what they are ready to face.

This is not what most of us were taught. We were taught that good support looks like comfort. Like warmth. Like taking the edge off. And so we absorb. We over-give. We reach for the thing that will ease their pain because we cannot tolerate watching them suffer.

But when we do that, we create crutches. We become the thing that stands between them and what they actually need to feel. The relief they experience is not liberation. It is dependency. And every time we rescue them from the discomfort, we take away the very moment that could have set them free.

That discomfort you feel when someone is in their pain? That is your work, not theirs.

A true frequency holder does not make people feel better. She holds the frequency long enough for them to go within and remember their own truth. Not a truth you handed them. Not a healing you gave them. Their own. The one that has always been there, waiting beneath everything they have been carrying.

That is the difference between a practitioner who creates dependency and one who creates liberation.

It starts with you doing exactly that for yourself first.

## THE FOUNDATION COMES FIRST

Most of us want the transformation without the foundation.

We want the expansion, the new identity, the version of ourselves that feels free and whole and fully expressed. So we go looking for it. We do the breathwork. We sit in ceremony. We invest in the program, the retreat, the healer. We read everything. We understand it intellectually. We feel glimpses of it. And then we collapse back.

We wonder why nothing sticks. Why we keep arriving at the same place. Why the insight never seems to become something we actually live. So we push harder. We try again. We go looking for the next thing that might finally be the thing.

Until eventually we stop. Exhausted. And somewhere in the quiet of that exhaustion a voice says what we have feared all along: Maybe I am just not someone who gets to have this. Maybe I am not good enough for this work. Maybe I am too far gone.

But that is not the truth.

The truth is we were setting ourselves up for failure from the beginning. Not because we were not committed enough, not because we did not want it badly enough, but because we tried to build something new on a foundation that was never rewired to hold it.

You cannot release what your nervous system does not feel safe to let go of. You cannot receive what your identity does not believe it is worthy of holding. You cannot integrate what your body has not been given the capacity to process. And you cannot sustain an expanded version of yourself if everything underneath is still running the old wiring.

The foundation is not the boring part. It is not the prerequisite you get through before the real work begins. It is the real work.

It is what determines whether the transformation you experience becomes something you actually live, or something that visits you briefly and then quietly leaves.

When we bypass the foundation we are not taking a shortcut. We are building on ground that will not hold. And the more we pile on top of it, the harder the collapse when it comes.

But when you do the foundation work first, something different happens. The nervous system starts to feel safe. The body starts to open. The old identity does not collapse in crisis — it dissolves gently, because there is finally something underneath it strong enough to hold what comes next.

You stop pushing and start receiving. You stop chasing the transformation and start becoming it.

She was never the problem. The ground beneath her just was not ready yet.

## YOUR WHY MUST BE BIGGER THAN YOUR PAIN

If your why is not bigger than your pain, you will always unconsciously choose comfortable.

And that is not a character flaw. It is not weakness. It is your nervous system and your mind doing exactly what they were designed to do — working together, protecting you, keeping you exactly where you are.

The mind will tell you the story. It will give you every reason why now is not the right time, why you need more information, why you should wait until things settle. And the nervous system will back it up. It will create the overwhelm, the procrastination, the sudden exhaustion, the feeling that you just cannot right now.

That is not you being lazy. That is a system that has kept you alive doing its job.

But here is the part we have to be honest about: that system was built for survival. Not for growth. Not for freedom. Not for the life you actually want.

If your reason for doing this work is only as big as the pain that brought you here, the moment that pain softens even slightly, you will stop. You will settle back into comfortable. And comfortable will feel like relief — until you realise it is the same place you have always been, just quieter.

Your why has to live deeper than that. It has to be something that calls you forward even when things feel okay. Even when you could justify stopping. Even when the nervous system is offering you every exit it knows.

## IF YOU ARE NOT WILLING TO SEE YOUR OWN PATTERNS

If you are not willing to see your own patterns, you will always find someone else to blame for why your life stays the same.

And I say that with love. Because I have been that person. Most of us have.

It is so much easier to look outward. To see what others are doing, what they are not giving us, how they are holding us back. Because the moment we turn inward and actually look at our patterns, we have to take responsibility for our own reality. And that is confronting in a way that nothing else is.

Comfortable shows up in so many forms. But underneath most of them is the same thing: the fear of being alone.

Because when we start to break our patterns, when we start to walk our truth and choose ourselves over adaptation, it can feel like we are being cast out. And that fear is not irrational. It is survival. To stay in a family system, a community, a tribe, there has always been an unspoken compliance. Believe what we believe. React how we react. Stay where we stay. And you belong.

When you start to change, that compliance breaks. And the people who were comfortable with the old version of you will feel it. Some will celebrate you. Some will pull away. Some will make it mean something about them.

And your nervous system will read all of that as danger. Because belonging kept you safe. And now you are choosing something that feels like isolation.

But here is the truth: if you are not willing to see your own patterns, you will always be manipulated by your own wounds. You will stay inside a version of connection that is built on who you used to be — on transactions that only work when you stay small, when you stay predictable, when you do not ask too much of the relationship or of yourself.

Truth seekers are pattern breakers. And pattern breaking will sometimes cost you relationships that only ever fit the old version of you.

So the question is not whether it will be uncomfortable. It will be. The question is whether you are really seeking truth — or whether you are staying safe inside a tribe you have already outgrown.
