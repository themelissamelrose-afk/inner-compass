## THE SOMATIC PROCESS — MELISSA'S TEACHING ON HOW TO GUIDE SOMEONE THROUGH A TRIGGER

I want to create some awareness around what they are feeling first. When someone shares a situation or circumstance, I like to validate that situation first. Maybe confirm what they are experiencing, then ask them: what does that trigger within them? What does that make them feel — what is their reaction to the circumstance, situation or person?

The reaction is the really important key. We need to address and get clear on the reaction, because the reaction is the portal back into the body. So if we're seeing the reaction — I'm really angry, I'm pissed off, I'm hurt, I feel betrayed — then we bring them back into the body.

Now we understand your reaction. It's valid to you. It doesn't mean it's right, but it's valid. If you are experiencing anger, validation creates acceptance.

Before we project our reaction, we bring presence and go within. We take self-responsibility first. Then we bring ourselves back into presence with the breath. We want the mind following the breath.

Take a nice deep breath in through the nose, deep breath out of the mouth. Do that three times. My feet are on the ground. My hands are beside me. We're bringing somatic awareness. We're bringing them into the present moment.

Now we address the feeling. When you feel angry — how does that feel? Because underneath the initial trigger there is a deeper feeling that lays underneath that. Then ask where does that feeling lay within your body? We're creating awareness of the feeling, bringing them back into the present moment, allowing them to find the sensation in their physical body.

And then, if they are calm enough, we ask: where have you felt this before?

Those are the steps. Sometimes we just have to get from the head to the heart and find acceptance that we're actually experiencing it, so we can view it from observation. Moving from reaction to responding differently.

A lot of people are stuck in their head so we need to work with what's visible first, observing their mind and the trigger first, then allowing them to bring awareness to the moment, then coming back to: what's the feeling under the trigger? Then giving them presence in the body. And if they can ask where they've felt this before, they can go a little deeper.

## THE DEEPER LAYERS OF SELF-ABANDONMENT

In the beginning, self-abandonment is obvious. Ignoring red flags. Staying when you're unhappy. Over-giving. People-pleasing. Accepting less than you deserve.

But as healing progresses, it becomes much more subtle. The next layer often looks like: doubting what you know, questioning your perception, giving old stories more authority than your present reality, looking outside yourself for confirmation when your body already knows, mistaking discomfort for a sign you're making the wrong decision.

The old thoughts still arise because they were wired into you for a long time. Healing doesn't necessarily stop the thoughts from appearing. It changes your relationship with them.

Instead of: "Maybe I'm asking too much." You now notice: "Ah, there's that old voice again."

Instead of: "Maybe I should stay." You notice: "I don't actually believe that anymore."

That is a huge difference.

What's beautiful about this deeper layer of healing is that you're not trying to get rid of disappointment. You're allowing disappointment without allowing it to dictate your choices. That's maturity.

For years, many women make decisions based on avoiding disappointment, loneliness, grief, rejection, or uncertainty. Eventually there comes a point where you realise: I can survive disappointment. What I don't want to survive anymore is abandoning myself.

Self-abandonment isn't always saying yes when you mean no.
Sometimes it's doubting yourself when you already know.
Sometimes it's negotiating with reality because the truth is inconvenient.
Sometimes it's giving old wounds a louder voice than present-day wisdom.

Healing isn't the absence of doubt. Healing is hearing the doubt and choosing yourself anyway.

The thoughts still visit. The wound still whispers. The disappointment still hurts. But you no longer hand them the steering wheel. You listen, acknowledge them, and then return to yourself.

That is a very different woman from the one who would have stayed hoping someone else would finally choose her. Now you're choosing yourself, even when it costs you something. And often, that's exactly where the deepest layer of healing lives.

## DAILY PRACTICES — BREATH AND PRESENCE THROUGH THE DAY

### 1. Morning Before You Even Open Your Eyes

Use the Sacred Six to tune into your body. Take a moment to just be there, notice any dreams, thoughts or feelings that are present as you wake. Don't try to change anything, just observe. Then use your exhale like a release, letting it all move through and out. From there, consciously inhale the day you want to begin with. Breathe in the energy of how you want to feel and gently meet the day ahead from that place.

### 2. Hourly Reminder — Breathe

Set a gentle alarm each hour that simply says Breathe. This is your moment to come back to yourself. We are interrupting the patterns of being lost in the mind, the distraction that keeps triggering your nervous system.

When the alarm goes off, pause. Notice the mind. What are you thinking? Just observe, see if you can witness what you're saying to yourself. If you can't yet, that's ok, you will in time.

Now take 3 deep breaths. Inhale through the nose, sigh it out the mouth. Bring yourself into your body.

My feet are on the ground — feel them.
My hands are beside me — feel them.

Now gently ask your body: what am I feeling? Just listen. And if something is there, let it be there. No fixing. No changing. Validate it. Allow yourself to be present with it. No judgement, just meeting yourself in presence. Without analysing. Without trying to work it out.

The feeling is just your current experience so allow it. Be with it.

If you feel nothing to begin with, that's ok too. Just let the feeling of presence itself calm your body.

This is how you stay grounded through your day, so you're not running on empty, and you begin to truly observe yourself.
